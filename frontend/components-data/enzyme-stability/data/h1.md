Hypothesis: a hydrogen-bond network around the active site stabilizes the catalytic conformation between pH 6.5 and 7.5. Below pH 6, protonation of His-residues collapses the network and inactivates the enzyme.

Key predictions:
- Loss of activity correlates with His protonation midpoint.
- Mutating His-148 → Ala flattens the pH–activity curve.
