## Question

Did the 3.5 Ga komatiites and associated chlorite-rich units of the Marble Bar greenstone belt undergo high-temperature seafloor hydrothermal alteration analogous to modern mid-ocean-ridge systems, or are the alteration mineralogy and chemistry better explained by later burial metamorphism?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Marble Bar locality (drill core DR-22 and selected outcrop sections).
- Komatiitic and basaltic units within the Warrawoona Group.
- Petrography, whole-rock geochemistry, mineral chemistry (EMPA), X-ray diffraction, and O-isotope geochemistry on chlorite + serpentine.

**Out of scope:**
- Younger sedimentary cover (handled by a separate project).
- Stratigraphic correlation across the entire Pilbara Craton — focus on the Marble Bar window.
- Microbial / biosignature interpretation.

## Success criteria

**Primary:** mass-balance and δ18O signatures of chlorite/serpentine that fall cleanly inside one of the two windows (modern MOR analogue *or* burial metamorphic). Mixed signals require a re-frame.

**Secondary:**
- Mineral assemblage textures consistent with the favoured fluid path.
- Cross-check against published Pilbara hydrothermal sequences (e.g. Kiyokawa 2012).

**Stretch:** quantitative T-ƒO2 estimate from EMPA chlorite thermobarometry that other workers can reproduce.

## Constraints

- **Time:** field season closes early Q3; lab work on returned samples must be complete before the dry season ends.
- **Budget:** AUD 90k Pilbara grant; covers fieldwork + lab time. EMPA + LA-ICP-MS booked through institutional rates.
- **Samples:** drill core DR-22 returned to base storage; field re-sampling possible only until 2026-06.
- **Hard "don't":** no destructive sampling of museum-archive specimens — use only project-collected material.
