**Hypothesis:** the chlorite + serpentine assemblage in the komatiites records hydrothermal alteration on the seafloor at temperatures of ~250–350 °C, analogous to modern slow-spreading MOR systems.

**Key predictions:**
- δ18O of chlorite within +5 to +9 ‰ (seawater-derived fluids at high T).
- Mass-balance Mg-loss + Si-gain in altered komatiites versus unaltered protoliths.
- Texturally early chlorite intergrown with primary olivine/clinopyroxene relics, *not* cross-cutting later veins.

**Status:** leading hypothesis. Petrographic evidence so far supports early chlorite. EMPA O-isotope batch pending.
